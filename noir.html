Our Process

The Art of
Creation

01
Conception
Vision & Sketch

Every NOIR piece begins with a single sketch — hand-drawn by our creative director at dawn, before the world intrudes. The vision must be pure before it can be built.

→
02
Material
Fabric Selection

We travel to mills in Lyon, Como, and Kyoto to source fabrics that no other house has access to. Our textiles are selected not just for beauty, but for how they hold shadow.

→
03
Construction
The Toile

Before a single thread of the final fabric is cut, our artisans construct a toile — a full prototype that may be deconstructed and rebuilt dozens of times.

→
04
Assembly
Hand-Finishing

Our master seamstresses finish every edge, every seam, every hem by hand. Under magnification. With the same tools used by the great couturiers of the 20th century.

→
05
Delivery
The Presentation

The finished garment is presented to the client in our Paris salon — lit by a single lamp, shown in silence, as it deserves to be. Fashion is theatre. This is the final act.

The Visionaries

🎭
Creative Director
Élise
Beaumont

A graduate of Esmod Paris, Élise reimagined NOIR at 34 with her debut collection "Cendres." Her work has been worn by heads of state, artists, and those who simply know.

🧵
Head of Couture
Marco
De Luca

Forty years in the atelier. Marco's hands have shaped couture for three generations. He is the last of his kind — a craftsman who learned the craft before computers existed.

💎
Head of Accessories
Yuki
Tanaka

From Kyoto to Paris, Yuki bridges wabi-sabi with European craft. Her accessories have been described as "objects that contain silence." She considers that high praise.
